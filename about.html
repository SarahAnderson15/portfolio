My Role at Rooster Sailing

I am a knowledgeable E-Commerce Manager overseeing multiple regional retail sites and trade portals for Rooster Sailing. We manage three retail websites hosted on Shopify, where I work with Liquid, CSS, and JavaScript to implement updates and new features based on the needs of the marketing team and Managing Director. Additionally, I manage our trade portals for the US and European dealer networks, ensuring product information and data are consistently updated. I am also responsible for maintaining our Amazon store. My previous experience in the customer service team has given me valuable insight into customer needs, enhancing my ability to create an effective and user-friendly online shopping experience.

Experience

I have been working in my current position since July 2023. During this time, I planned and led the transition to a brand-new theme for the Rooster Sailing retail sites. This included implementing custom coding and collaborating with a more experienced outsourced web development company. I also liaised with our SEO specialist team to ensure the new site incorporated all the essential elements for Google search success. It was during this process that my passion and interest in web development grew, leading me to pursue further learning and enroll in a web development course.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, I was furloughed from my job as a flight attendant due to lockdowns. During this time, I worked at a vaccination center, handling administrative tasks and inputting vaccine data into the NHS system. After two months, I was promoted to a non-clinical lead position, where I oversaw the center's operations, led the administrative team, and assisted with data entry and technical issues.

Education

At school, I enjoyed my IT and business classes. As someone who wasn’t particularly academic, these were the subjects where I thrived—building small websites and recreating existing ones. However, after school, I followed advice from others who believed I was only suited for certain careers. I initially went to college for cooking, as I had a passion for it, but turning it into a job took the fun out of it, and I quickly realized it wasn’t the career I wanted to pursue. Next, I was encouraged to try aviation, with the promise of traveling the world for cheap. That led me to study aviation and eventually land a job with EasyJet. While I gained valuable experience, the work felt repetitive—performing checks, boarding passengers, dealing with complaints beyond my control, serving drinks and snacks, landing, and repeating the process. It was during this time that I realized I needed to take control of my own career path. I decided to enroll in a Business A-Level that I could complete while working. I took my exams in October 2020 and passed with one of my highest grades, which helped me secure a job at Rooster. Since then, I’ve worked hard and grown within the company, and I’m now learning front-end development with Codecademy to expand my skill set.
